# Daggerheart Primer

An unofficial, non-commercial primer and study repository for **Daggerheart™**, the fantasy tabletop roleplaying game by [Darrington Press](https://darringtonpress.com/daggerheart/) (the publishing arm of Critical Role). This repo collects notes, references, and links to official materials for the purpose of **learning and exploring the game**. All game content belongs to its creators — see [LICENSE](LICENSE).

- **Official site:** https://www.daggerheart.com
- **Designers:** Spenser Starke & Rowan Hall
- **Publisher:** Darrington Press (Critical Role)
- **Released:** May 20, 2025

## What Is Daggerheart?

Daggerheart is a fiction-first, heroic fantasy TTRPG built around collaborative storytelling. Where many fantasy games emphasize tactical grids and rigid turn order, Daggerheart emphasizes narrative momentum, shared worldbuilding, and an emotional engine baked directly into the dice: **Hope and Fear**.

Key design pillars:

- **Fiction first** — the story drives the mechanics, not the other way around.
- **The Duality Dice** — every action roll tells you not just *whether* you succeed, but *how it feels*.
- **No initiative order** — the spotlight flows naturally through the scene instead of a fixed turn sequence.
- **Card-based character options** — domain abilities, ancestries, communities, and subclasses are presented as cards for easy table use.
- **Long-term campaign play** — characters advance across 10 levels in 4 tiers, with meaningful choices at every level.

## Core Mechanics

### The Duality Dice (2d12)

Every action roll uses two twelve-sided dice of different colors: the **Hope Die** and the **Fear Die**. Roll both, add your relevant trait modifier, and compare to a Difficulty set by the GM.

| Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Hope die is higher | You roll **with Hope** — you gain a Hope token |
| Fear die is higher | You roll **with Fear** — the GM gains a Fear token |
| Both dice match | **Critical success!** You succeed with a bonus, gain a Hope, and clear a Stress |

Success and failure are independent of Hope/Fear, producing four dramatic outcomes: *success with Hope*, *success with Fear*, *failure with Hope*, and *failure with Fear*. Even failure moves the story forward.

### Hope & Fear Economy

- **Hope** (players, max 6 each) is spent to help an ally, use an Experience, power class "Hope Features," and more.
- **Fear** (GM) is spent to interrupt the players, spotlight adversaries, and activate dangerous environment and adversary features. It's the engine of escalating tension.

### Character Traits

Six traits modify your rolls: **Agility, Strength, Finesse, Instinct, Presence, Knowledge**. Characters also have freeform **Experiences** (e.g., *"Ex-Soldier"*, *"Raised by Wolves"*) that can be invoked with Hope for a bonus.

### Damage, Stress & Armor

- Incoming damage is compared against your **damage thresholds** (Minor / Major / Severe) to determine how many **Hit Points** you mark (1, 2, or 3).
- **Stress** tracks strain and exhaustion; many abilities cost Stress, and some effects force you to mark it.
- **Armor slots** can be spent to reduce incoming damage.
- **Evasion** is the difficulty adversaries must beat to hit you.

### Death Moves

When a character falls, the player chooses how the story goes: go out in a **Blaze of Glory**, **Avoid Death** at a cost, or **Risk It All** on one final roll.

### Rests & Downtime

Short and long rests let players clear HP, Stress, and armor slots, and prepare — while giving the GM resources too. Downtime keeps the pressure of the fiction alive even between adventures.

## Character Creation

A character combines a **Class** (with **Subclass**), an **Ancestry**, a **Community**, traits, and a loadout of **Domain Cards**.

### The 9 Classes & Their Domains

Each class draws from two of the nine domains; every domain is shared by two classes.

| Class | Domains | Subclasses |
|---|---|---|
| **Bard** | Grace + Codex | Troubadour, Wordsmith |
| **Druid** | Sage + Arcana | Warden of the Elements, Warden of Renewal |
| **Guardian** | Valor + Blade | Stalwart, Vengeance |
| **Ranger** | Bone + Sage | Beastbound, Wayfinder |
| **Rogue** | Midnight + Grace | Nightwalker, Syndicate |
| **Seraph** | Splendor + Valor | Divine Wielder, Winged Sentinel |
| **Sorcerer** | Arcana + Midnight | Elemental Origin, Primal Origin |
| **Warrior** | Blade + Bone | Call of the Brave, Call of the Slayer |
| **Wizard** | Codex + Splendor | School of Knowledge, School of War |

### The 9 Domains

The core set includes **189 unique domain cards** (abilities and spells) across these domains:

- **Arcana** — raw, instinctual magic
- **Blade** — mastery of weapons and slaughter
- **Bone** — tactics, the body, and battlefield awareness
- **Codex** — studied, learned magic
- **Grace** — charm, performance, and manipulation
- **Midnight** — shadows, stealth, and subterfuge
- **Sage** — the natural world and its power
- **Splendor** — light, healing, and divinity
- **Valor** — protection and unbreakable defense

### Ancestries

Clank, Drakona, Dwarf, Elf, Faerie, Faun, Firbolg, Fungril, Galapa, Giant, Goblin, Halfling, Human, Infernis, Katari, Orc, Ribbet, Simian — plus rules for **Mixed Ancestry**. Each ancestry grants two features.

### Communities

Where you come from matters as much as what you are: **Highborne, Loreborne, Orderborne, Ridgeborne, Seaborne, Slyborne, Underborne, Wanderborne, Wildborne**.

### Advancement

Characters advance from level 1 to 10 across four tiers (1, 2–4, 5–7, 8–10), gaining new domain cards, trait increases, subclass upgrades, and optional **multiclassing** at higher tiers.

## Running the Game (GM)

- The GM uses **Fear** and **GM moves** instead of a fixed action economy.
- **Adversaries** are built with roles (Bruiser, Horde, Leader, Minion, Ranged, Skulk, Solo, Social, Standard, Support) and tiered stat blocks.
- **Environments** get their own stat blocks with features and countdowns.
- **Countdowns** (progress clocks) pace dramatic events.
- **Campaign Frames** are ready-to-run campaign premises with tone, lore hooks, session-zero guidance, and custom mechanics.
